Privacy Policy

Governing the collection and use of biometric identifiers

Policy version 1.0  ·  Effective date: April 14, 2026  ·  Last updated: April 14, 2026

1. Introduction

chickenScratch is a product of Orchestrate LLC ("we", "our", "the Service"). We provide handwriting-based biometric authentication to businesses ("Customers") whose end users ("Users") enroll and verify their identity through our SDK or API.

Because we collect, store, and process biometric identifiers, this policy describes in plain language exactly what we collect, how we protect it, how long we keep it, and what rights you have over it. We take these obligations seriously.

2. What Is Biometric Data?

Biometric data is information derived from a person's unique physical or behavioral characteristics. The following biometric identifiers and information are collected by chickenScratch:

Data type Description Purpose
Handwriting strokes Raw pen/touch stroke trajectories (X/Y coordinates, timestamps) captured while signing your name Identity enrollment & verification
Drawing strokes Stroke trajectories captured while drawing standard shapes (circle, square, triangle, house, smiley face) Multi-factor biometric verification
Pressure data Stylus/touch pressure readings during drawing, where the device supports it Liveness & forgery detection
Timing features Inter-stroke pauses, drawing rhythm, stroke duration patterns Behavioral biometric scoring
Geometric features Derived mathematical features: curvature, direction changes, spatial efficiency Identity scoring
Device fingerprint Non-identifying device signals: screen resolution, GPU renderer, timezone, language. No cookies, no IP stored permanently. Replay-attack detection
What we do NOT collect: we do not collect facial images, voice recordings, fingerprints, retina scans, DNA, or any biometric data beyond handwriting and drawing behavior described above.

3. How We Collect Biometric Data

Biometric data is collected through the chickenScratch JavaScript SDK or API, which is embedded in a Customer's application. Collection happens:

  • During enrollment: you draw your signature 3 times and draw 5 standard shapes. These samples are used to build your unique biometric baseline.
  • During verification: you draw your signature once and the 5 shapes in a randomized order. The attempt is compared to your baseline.

No biometric data is collected passively or in the background. Collection only occurs when you explicitly interact with the drawing canvas.

4. Explicit Consent

We collect biometric data only with your explicit, informed, written consent. Before any data is collected, you will be presented with a consent notice that explains:

  • What biometric data will be collected
  • The specific purpose (authentication only)
  • A link to this full privacy policy
  • The right to decline or withdraw consent at any time

Your consent is recorded with a timestamp, your IP address, and the policy version you agreed to. You may withdraw consent at any time — see Section 8 for details.

5. How We Use Biometric Data

We use biometric data for one purpose only: to verify that the person attempting to authenticate is the same person who enrolled.

We do not:

  • Sell biometric data to any third party
  • Share biometric data with advertising networks or data brokers
  • Use biometric data to train general machine learning models
  • Transfer biometric data across international borders without appropriate safeguards
  • Use biometric data for any purpose beyond authentication for the specific Customer application where you enrolled

6. Security Measures

We implement the following technical safeguards:

  • Encryption at rest: all biometric feature data is encrypted using AES-256-GCM with a unique IV per record before being written to the database.
  • Encryption in transit: all data is transmitted over HTTPS (TLS 1.2+). Plain HTTP connections are rejected.
  • Access control: biometric data is partitioned by tenant. Customer A cannot access Customer B's user data, and vice versa.
  • Score leakage lockdown: the API returns only pass/fail. Raw scores and biometric features are never returned to clients.
  • Replay protection: every verification challenge uses a one-time token with a 5-minute expiry and randomized drawing order.
  • No plaintext storage: raw stroke data is encrypted immediately upon receipt.

7. Data Retention

Data Retention period
Biometric enrollment samples & baselines Until the User requests deletion, the Customer terminates their account, or 3 years of inactivity — whichever comes first
Verification attempt records 90 days (used for fraud detection and security auditing), then anonymized
Consent records 7 years (legal requirement for demonstrating compliance)
Device fingerprint signals Stored alongside the attempt record — same 90-day retention

Upon deletion, all biometric identifiers and derived features are permanently and irreversibly destroyed. Anonymized aggregate statistics (e.g., "10,000 authentications processed this month") may be retained indefinitely.

8. Your Rights

Illinois BIPA (Biometric Information Privacy Act)

If you are an Illinois resident, you have the right to:

  • Be informed before biometric data is collected (fulfilled by this policy and the consent step)
  • Know the specific purpose and length of storage
  • Have your biometric data destroyed upon request or upon the earlier of: fulfillment of the initial purpose, or within 3 years of your last interaction
  • Not have your biometric data sold or disclosed without consent
  • Sue for damages if your rights under BIPA are violated

Texas CUBI (Capture or Use of Biometric Identifier Act)

If you are a Texas resident, we comply with the Texas CUBI statute by: obtaining informed consent before collection, using data only for the stated purpose, storing data securely, and destroying data within a reasonable time after the purpose is fulfilled or within 1 year of your last interaction.

GDPR (EU/UK residents)

Biometric data is "special category" data under GDPR Article 9. Our lawful basis for processing is explicit consent (Article 9(2)(a)). You have the right to:

  • Access: request a copy of your biometric data and processing records
  • Rectification: correct inaccurate data
  • Erasure ("right to be forgotten"): request permanent deletion of all biometric data
  • Restriction: restrict processing while a complaint is investigated
  • Portability: receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Objection: object to processing at any time — we will stop immediately
  • Withdraw consent: withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing

How to exercise your rights

Contact the Customer whose application you enrolled in — they are the data controller responsible for your data. Alternatively, contact chickenScratch directly at the address in Section 10. We will respond within 30 days. Deletion requests are fulfilled within 72 hours.

9. Third-Party Disclosure

We do not sell, lease, trade, or otherwise disclose biometric data to third parties, except:

  • Infrastructure providers: our database host (Railway) processes data on our behalf under a Data Processing Agreement. They are prohibited from accessing or using your data for any purpose other than providing infrastructure services.
  • Legal requirement: if required by a valid court order or applicable law, we will notify the affected Customer before disclosure where permitted by law.

No advertising networks, analytics companies, or data brokers ever receive biometric data.
